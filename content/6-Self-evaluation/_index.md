# Self-Evaluation

## Introduction

During my participation in the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program, I took on the role of **Team Leader** - responsible for coordinating team tasks, making technical decisions, ensuring project progress, and supporting team members throughout the workshop.

Looking back on the entire journey, I would like to honestly evaluate myself across 8 key criteria, reflecting on both what I achieved and where I still have room to grow.

---

## Evaluation Table

| No. | Criteria | Rating | Comments |
|-----|----------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **Knowledge** | Good | Before joining FCAJ, I had some basic knowledge of AWS services. During the program, I was able to apply services such as S3, AWS Glue, Amazon Athena, EC2, VPC, and CloudWatch in a cohesive end-to-end architecture. Understanding the Medallion Data Lakehouse concept and implementing it hands-on deepened my cloud knowledge significantly. |
| 2 | **Learning Ability** |  Good | I encountered many new services and concepts for the first time - Data Firehose, Glue Data Catalog auto-registration, Streamlit on EC2. I was able to quickly read documentation, search for solutions, and apply them in practice. I enjoy the process of researching independently before asking for help. |
| 3 | **Proactiveness** |  Good | As team leader, I did not wait for instructions - I took the initiative in proposing the architecture, dividing tasks among members, setting up the S3 structure, writing the ETL scripts, and troubleshooting when jobs failed. I also proactively prepared workshop documentation to support the team's presentation. |
| 4 | **Discipline** |  Fair | In terms of discipline, I generally kept up with the program schedule and meeting deadlines for deliverables. However, I acknowledge that there were times when I underestimated task complexity, which caused minor delays. Going forward, I want to improve my time estimation and planning habits. |
| 5 | **Communication** |  Fair | Communicating technical concepts clearly - whether to teammates with different skill levels or to mentors - is something I am still developing. I could explain system flows well, but sometimes I was too detailed in a way that created confusion rather than clarity. I plan to practice presenting more concisely. |

| 7 | **Problem Solving** |  Good | The project involved real debugging challenges - Glue job path mismatches, Athena table registration issues, Streamlit not loading due to security group configuration. | 8 | **Contribution to Project** |  Good | I contributed to the majority of technical deliverables: designing the architecture and writing both the English and Vietnamese workshop documentation. |

## Areas for Improvement

1. **Discipline & Time Management** - I need to get better at breaking down large tasks into smaller milestones and tracking them consistently. Using tools like Notion or GitHub Projects would help.

2. **Communication** - Especially when presenting technical content to non-technical audiences. I want to practice the "what, why, how" structure to make explanations more accessible and concise.

3. **Delegation** - As team leader, I sometimes tried to handle too many things myself instead of trusting teammates with ownership. Effective delegation is a leadership skill I want to develop further.

---

## Reflection

Taking on the team leader role in FCAJ was challenging but incredibly rewarding. I learned that leadership is not just about technical skill - it requires patience, clear communication, the ability to motivate others, and sometimes the humility to say "I don't know, let's figure it out together."

The project we built - a serverless Medallion Data Lakehouse on AWS - gave me real, hands-on confidence with cloud architecture that I would not have gained simply from reading tutorials. I am grateful for the opportunity and proud of what the team accomplished together.
